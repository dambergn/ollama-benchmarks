## Prompt
```
Tell me about the speed of light.
```

## Response
```
Here's a breakdown of the fascinating topic of the speed of light:

**What is it?**

The speed of light in a vacuum (often represented by the letter 'c') is a fundamental physical constant, 
approximately **299,792,458 meters per second**. This means light travels nearly 300,000 kilometers (186,000 
miles) every second!

**Why is it so important?**

* **Universal Speed Limit:**  Einstein's theory of special relativity states that nothing in the universe can 
travel faster than the speed of light. This has profound implications for our understanding of space, time, 
and gravity.
* **Defining Distance:** Astronomers use the speed of light to calculate distances in the universe. Since it 
takes time for light to travel from distant stars and galaxies, measuring how long that light has been 
traveling allows us to determine how far away those objects are.

**Does light always travel at this speed?**

No! The speed of light is constant only in a vacuum. When light travels through other materials like air, 
water, or glass, it slows down. This is because the light interacts with the atoms in the material, causing 
it to scatter and change direction.

**Interesting Facts:**

* Light takes about 8 minutes to travel from the Sun to Earth.
* The speed of light is so fast that it's essentially instantaneous for most everyday purposes.
* Scientists are constantly trying to measure the speed of light with even greater precision.
```
