speed of light itself can vary slightly under varying conditions.

It's also worth mentioning that the concept of speed of light is not absolute in nature but rather relative. 
In a vacuum where no medium is present, the speed of light remains constant at 299,792,458 meters per second, 
regardless of other factors like temperature or distance from us. This means that if we were to go faster 
than this speed by a small amount (e.g., moving a few kilometers over a short period), our velocity would 
still be measured as 299,792,458 m/s.

Remember, for practical purposes and in everyday contexts, you can often think of the speed of light as 
approximately 300,000 meters per second, which is a common approximation used in many scientific and 
engineering fields.
